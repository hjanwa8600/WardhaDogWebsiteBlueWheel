A few words about us

Wardha Dog Rescue provides food, sanctuary and medical aid to the strays in Wardha District and educates the public about stray dog issues through innovative storytelling and campaigning to foster a positive attitude towards these animals. We have over 80 rescued dogs. We also provide food, shelter, and medical assistance to over 50 dogs in Sawangi.

Our Mission

To provide food, sanctuary and medical assistance to 10,000 dogs in Wardha District by 2025 and reach over a billion people by leveraging print and social media to educate them on the realities of life on the streets for stray dogs and create a new understanding of stray dogs and the issues surrounding them.

Our Vision

A world where stray dogs live and die with dignity.

Our Team
Manisha Hariharan – Founder

Entrepreneur and animal activist with over 10 years of experience in business and even longer in animal welfare, Manisha Hariharan is skilled in multiple lines of work in the realms of travel, training, recruitment, sales, transcription and editing. She currently runs a language solutions firm and founded Wardha Dog Rescue as an expression of her passion for both business and animals. A former People for Animals member and volunteer, she is coached and guided by Mrs. Maneka Gandhi. She has rescued and rehabilitated hundreds of animals over her lifetime. While volunteering with PFA Wardha, she handled corporate and media communications, drafted project proposals, press releases, newsletters, annual reports and content for promotional material. She authored a column called “Animals And All” on animal welfare and related issues in Vidarbha Herald, a weekly newspaper based in Chandrapur. She was also a guest blogger at People for Animals. She has liaisoned with government officials and politicians, raised funds for key projects such as Animal Birth Control Program, Goshala and Wildlife Enclosures, organized first aid camps, workshop for SHARAN India in association with People for Animals and Department of Obstetrics, JNMC, Datta Meghe Institute of Medical Sciences. She co-ordinated BBD Event (Big Bird Day) jointly with Delhi birders and launched public awareness campaigns on street dogs. Her endeavor is to build cruelty-free businesses to set up the largest Animal Welfare Fund in India and the business community to further the cause of animal welfare.

Jigna Keshav - Trustee

Jigna Keshav is an alumni of Tata Institute of Social Sciences, having completed her Masters in Social work in 2009. She has worked extensively in the field of health and education, in both urban and rural areas in the past decade and continues to do so. Our Local NGO Partners

People for Animals Wardha unit is an oasis for animals in central Maharashtra. It was registered as a charitable trust in 2000, and is recognized by the AWBI. The organization runs an animal shelter and a wildlife rescue and rehabilitation center recognized by the Central Zoo Authority. Karunashram, the animal shelter spread over 15 acres is testimony to their commitment to Baba’s message. The facility is permanent home for numerous cattle, dogs, pigs, and other street animals who are unable to fend for themselves.
PFA Wardha is the authorized NGO for Animal Birth Control program with the Wardha Municipality. With more than 10,000 successful ABC surgeries till date, PFA Wardha has set the benchmark for effective ABC in the state.
PFA Wardha actively engages with the local forest department to rescue and rehabilitate wild animals in distress. Their expertise in the field has received the stamp of approval from the CZA recently. After treatment, the animals are handed over to the officials for rehabilitation in the wild. Presently, the center has nilgai (blue bull), barn owls, snakes, civets and peafowl undergoing treatment.
We are indebted to them for use of their facilities and inputs.

Our Advisory
Smt. Maneka Gandhi

Maneka Gandhi is the founder of People for Animals and is currently the chairperson of this organization. Whilst serving India as a political member of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), Maneka Gandhi founded India’s largest organization for animal rights and welfare, People for Animals. Following the foundation of this organization, Maneka Gandhi was similarly appointed chairperson of the Committee for the Purpose and Control and Supervision of Experiments on Animals (CPCSEA) in 1995. Throughout her time as an animal rights activist, Maneka Gandhi has been involved in investigating laboratories where animal testing was being carried out, has filed for Animal Birth Control programs (ABCs) in order to limit the number of homeless dogs that were killed as a result of overpopulation and has also been involved in initiatives planned by the organization International Animal Rescue. Maneka Gandhi is our inspiration and advises us on all aspects of animal welfare
